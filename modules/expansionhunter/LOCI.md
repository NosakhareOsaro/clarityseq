## Notes on Specific Loci

### FMR1 (CGG) — Loci 14, 15, 34, 58
The same locus produces four distinct clinical syndromes depending on repeat
count and sex of carrier:
- ≤44 repeats: normal
- 45–54: grey zone (uncertain significance)
- 55–200 (pre-mutation):
  - Males: FXTAS (fragile X-associated tremor/ataxia syndrome, OMIM 300623)
  - Females: FXPOI (primary ovarian insufficiency, OMIM 311360)
- ≥200 (full mutation):
  - Males and females: Fragile X syndrome (FRAXA, OMIM 300624)

### RFC1 (loci 23, 36, 39) — Pathogenic Motif Switch
The reference allele at RFC1 contains AAAAG repeats. Pathogenic CANVAS
requires biallelic expansion of the **AAGGG** motif (not the reference
AAAAG). ExpansionHunter v5.0 with the v5.0 catalog genotypes all three
common RFC1 motifs: AAAAG (benign), AAGGG (pathogenic), and AAAGG (rare
uncertain). Short-read WGS cannot always phase compound heterozygotes;
long-read confirmation is recommended when biallelic AAGGG is suspected.

### D4Z4 / FSHD1 (locus 29)
D4Z4 units are 3.3 kb each — far too large for short-read spanning. ExpansionHunter
estimates D4Z4 count by read-depth modelling of the repeat array. Southern
blot or optical mapping is required for definitive FSHD1 diagnosis.
ExpansionHunter output should be treated as SCREENING ONLY for this locus.

### BAFME loci (25–27, 41–43) — TTTCA insertion
BAFME/FAME loci involve insertion of a pathogenic TTTCA motif within a
TTTTA array. ExpansionHunter v5.0 uses a dual-motif catalog entry to detect
both motifs. The TTTCA insertion count (not total repeat length) determines
pathogenicity.

### SCA36 (NOP56, GGCCTG) — Locus 20
SCA36 involves a hexanucleotide (GGCCTG) expansion. ExpansionHunter handles
hexanucleotide motifs but read-spanning is less reliable than for trinucleotide
repeats. Treat calls >14 repeats as screening-positive requiring confirmatory
RP-PCR or Southern blot.
